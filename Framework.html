A gentle, self-paced way through the four movements at the heart of Harmonee — to come back to yourself, a little at a time.

Before you begin

How to use this

  • Go at your own pace. There's no order you have to follow and nothing to finish.
  • You can stop at any point. Closing the page is always allowed.
  • Take what fits and leave what doesn't. You know yourself best.
  • This is educational and reflective — it isn't therapy or crisis support.

Phase 01

Pause

Before anything changes, there's a pause — the small gap between what happens and what we do next. Pausing isn't doing nothing; it's making room.

You might notice

A pull to react quickly, to fix or explain. A breath that's higher and shorter than usual. The sense that there's no time — when often there is a little more than it feels.

A small practice

Name one thing you can see and one thing you can hear, slowly. That's the whole practice. You've already paused.

Phase 02

Regulate

Regulation isn't about feeling calm on command. It's about helping your body feel a little safer, so the rest has room to settle.

You might notice

Tension that lives somewhere specific — jaw, shoulders, stomach. Energy that feels too high or strangely flat. Both are information, not failure.

A small practice

Let your exhale be a little longer than your inhale, a few times. Longer out-breaths gently tell the body it's okay to ease.

Phase 03

Reconnect

Once there's a little more steadiness, we can turn gently back toward ourselves — toward what we feel, need, and care about.

You might notice

A feeling that's been waiting to be named. A need that got set aside. Something that matters to you, quietly asking for attention.

A small practice

Finish this once, honestly: "Right now, I could use a little more ______." No need to act on it yet — just let it be true.

Phase 04

Return

Returning is carrying a little of this back into an ordinary day — not as a grand change, but as one small, doable thing.

You might notice

The urge to overhaul everything at once. Gently, smaller is more sustainable. One step you'd actually take beats ten you won't.

A small practice

Choose one kind, realistic thing for the next hour. Just the next hour. You can choose again after that.

A soft close

Returning, again and again

This isn't a path you complete once. Most of us move through these four many times, in big and small ways. Coming back to the beginning isn't starting over — it's how the practice works.
